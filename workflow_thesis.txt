================================================================================
        FARM CONNECT — END-TO-END WORKFLOW THESIS
        SBR Agrotech / Sigmoyd | Prepared: July 2026
================================================================================

PURPOSE
───────
This document traces the single, continuous workflow of the system — from the
moment a budget is first conceived to the moment a crop is growing on a
mapped, labour-allocated, equipment-supported plot of land. It is the
operational backbone that the rest of the platform (approvals, ledgers,
dashboards) is built around.

The workflow runs through 9 stages:

  1. Budget Creation (Capex + Opex)
  2. Amortization (for Capex)
  3. Cash Flow Projection
  4. Finalized Budget with Line-Item Working
  5. Land Onboarding (Leads → KYC → Registration)
  6. HR Onboarding (MRF under Budget Header)
  7. Equipment / Inventory Onboarding (19-step Procurement Cycle)
  8. Vehicle Onboarding
  9. Scope of Work, Labour Distribution, Cultivation Master → Plan → Calendar
     → On-Demand Tasks

Each stage gates the next. Nothing downstream can happen without the upstream
stage existing — this is what makes the platform a chain rather than a set of
disconnected modules.



════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
STAGE 1 — BUDGET CREATION: CAPEX + OPEX
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The workflow begins at the Director/Finance level, not in the field.

  • The user creates a budget and splits every planned cost into two buckets:
      CAPEX (Capital Expenditure) — one-time, asset-creating spend
        e.g. tractors, borewells, sheds, vehicles, heavy equipment
      OPEX (Operating Expenditure) — recurring, season-to-season spend
        e.g. seeds, labour, fertilizers, fuel, rentals

  • Each line item carries: S.No, Item Name, UOM, Quantity, Per-Unit Cost,
    and a computed Amount (Quantity × Per-Unit Cost).

  • Capex and Opex are tracked as two parallel tables, each rolling up to a
    Total Capex and Total Opex figure for the project.

This is the seed of every cost figure that appears anywhere else in the
system — the Indent, the Purchase Order, the Ledger entry, and the per-acre
investment figure all trace their lineage back to a line item created here.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

WHY THIS MATTERS
  Without separating Capex from Opex at the point of budgeting, a company
  cannot correctly amortize its capital spend or distinguish a one-time asset
  purchase from a recurring operating cost — both of which have completely
  different accounting treatments and cash flow implications.



════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
STAGE 2 — AMORTIZATION (TRIGGERED BY CAPEX)
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The moment a Capex line item is added, it becomes eligible to be pulled into
an Amortization schedule. The user selects one or more Capex items (a
"bifurcation") and configures:

  1. Loan Amount (CAPEX)        — the asset cost being financed
  2. Interest (Rate %)          — annual interest rate
  3. Loan Period                — total repayment duration
  4. Moratorium                 — months before repayment begins
  5. No. of Months in a Year    — repayment frequency basis (usually 12)
  6. Repayment Period           — tenure over which EMI is calculated

From these six inputs, the system computes a full month-by-month amortization
table:

  Month | Year | Opening Balance | Interest | Principal | EMI | Closing Balance

Each row reduces the opening balance by the principal component until the
loan is fully repaid. This schedule becomes the source of EMI obligations
that the Director tracks under EMIs & Investments, and it directly feeds
Stage 3 (Cash Flow) as a recurring monthly outflow.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

WHY THIS MATTERS
  Capex is rarely paid in cash upfront at this scale — assets like tractors,
  borewells, and processing equipment are financed. Amortization converts a
  single lump-sum Capex figure into a monthly cash obligation that can be
  planned against revenue. Without this step, cash flow projection would be
  blind to debt servicing.



════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
STAGE 3 — CASH FLOW PROJECTION
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Once Capex, Opex, and the Amortization schedule all exist, the system
generates a Cash Flow grid. Each row in the grid is a financial signal,
projected month-by-month:

  • Capex Outflow            — pulled from Stage 1
  • Opex Outflow             — pulled from Stage 1
  • EMI Obligation           — pulled from Stage 2 (amortization)
  • Cycle Revenue            — expected income per crop cycle
  • Inflow / Outflow / Net   — per month
  • Cumulative Balance       — running cash position

Opex can be modeled on different bases (per-acre, per-750-acres, fixed
annual) and Capex similarly (per-acre, per-unit, fixed) — giving Finance the
flexibility to scale projections as land under management grows.

This is the first point in the workflow where the company can answer:
"If we commit to this Capex and this Opex, will we run out of cash before
the first harvest revenue arrives?"

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

WHY THIS MATTERS
  Agriculture has a structural cash flow problem: costs are front-loaded
  (land prep, seeds, labour) and revenue arrives only after harvest, months
  later. At 12,000 acres, a cash flow miscalculation can stall operations
  mid-season. This stage is the company's early-warning system.



════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
STAGE 4 — FINALIZED BUDGET WITH LINE-ITEM WORKING
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

With Capex, Opex, Amortization, and Cash Flow validated, the budget is
finalized into its working form — the version that actual operational spend
will be measured against.

Each finalized line item carries a "working" triplet that tracks its
lifecycle in real time:

  • SAVINGS DONE          — difference between budgeted rate/qty and the
                             actual rate achieved through vendor negotiation
                             (realized the moment a PO is issued below the
                             budgeted rate)
  • AMOUNT IN PIPELINE     — value of POs/Indents raised against this line
                             item that are approved but not yet fully paid
                             or closed (in-flight commitments)
  • TOTAL UTILIZED BUDGET  — cumulative amount actually spent and ledgered
                             against this line item

A finalized budget line also carries: Category, UOM, Qty per Acre, Total
Acres, Rate per Unit — so that every downstream Indent can be checked against
both a quantity ceiling and a per-acre cost ceiling.

This is the budget object that every Indent in Stage 7 selects from.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

WHY THIS MATTERS
  A budget that is only a plan is not a control. The Savings / Pipeline /
  Utilized triplet turns the budget into a live financial control surface:
  Finance can see, line by line, how much headroom remains before a category
  is exhausted — before the money is committed, not after.



════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
STAGE 5 — LAND ONBOARDING (LEADS → VERIFICATION → KYC → REGISTRATION)
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

With budget headroom confirmed, land acquisition begins. This runs through
the Leads module:

  Step 1 — LEAD CAPTURE
    Field agent records a prospective land/farmer contact: name, phone,
    village, estimated land area, water availability.

  Step 2 — VERIFICATION
    Field agent confirms the lead is genuine and the land is real.
    Status moves: contacted → verified (or follow_up / rejected).

  Step 3 — KYC & REGISTRATION
    Full legal onboarding: Aadhaar, PAN, bank account + IFSC, lease
    agreement dates, rent amount, B1 record, Kisan book, agreement PDF,
    profile photo. Status moves: verified → registered.

  COMPLICATION HANDLED — OWNERSHIP TOPOLOGY
    Real land records in India rarely map 1:1 to a single farmer. The system
    is built to handle both directions of this complexity:
      (a) ONE LAND PARCEL, MULTIPLE OWNERS — a single plot is jointly owned;
          the KYC and lease agreement must capture and bind multiple owner
          KYC records to one land parcel, with rent/payment splits.
      (b) ONE OWNER, MULTIPLE LAND PARCELS — a single farmer leases out
          several disconnected plots; the system links multiple farm_ids
          back to one farmer_id without duplicating KYC data.
    This is why the data model separates Farmer (the legal/KYC identity)
    from Farm (the physical land parcel) as distinct, many-to-many-capable
    entities rather than collapsing them into one record.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

WHY THIS MATTERS
  Land ownership disputes are the single largest legal risk in scaled
  agri-leasing. Correctly modeling multi-owner and multi-parcel relationships
  at the data layer — rather than forcing every farm into a single-owner
  assumption — is what makes 12,000 acres of leased land legally defensible.



════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
STAGE 6 — HR ONBOARDING (MRF UNDER BUDGET HEADER)
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

With land coming online, the workforce to operate it must be staffed. This is
not an open-ended hiring process — it is tied directly back to Stage 4's
budget.

  Step 1 — MRF CREATION (Man Power Requisition Form)
    HR raises an MRF against a SPECIFIC budget line item (the labour/staffing
    head defined in Stage 1/4). The MRF captures: department, sub-department,
    target date, impact level, contract type (Permanent/Temporary), reason
    for vacancy, and a billing grid — designation, age limit, quantity,
    job description, department, CTC per role — rolling up to a Total CTC
    and Grand Total cost.

  Step 2 — TWO-LEVEL APPROVAL
    HR submits to Admin Ops first → Admin Ops reviews and signs →
    forwarded to Director for final approval. Both approval stamps are
    captured against the MRF number.

  Step 3 — BUDGET ALLOCATION & ONBOARDING
    Once both approvals clear, the budgeted amount for that line item is
    allocated against the MRF's estimated CTC. HR then begins actual
    onboarding (Staff Onboarding module) strictly within that allocated
    envelope — designation by designation, against the MRF quantities.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

WHY THIS MATTERS
  Tying every hire back to a specific budget line item (rather than a general
  HR headcount plan) prevents staffing cost creep — the most common silent
  leakage in large agricultural operations. The 2-level sign-off mirrors the
  financial discipline already applied to procurement.



════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
STAGE 7 — EQUIPMENT / INVENTORY ONBOARDING
            (THE 19-STEP PROCUREMENT-TO-CLOSURE CYCLE)
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

This is the most elaborate and tightly governed workflow in the entire
system. Every piece of equipment, consumable, or service the operation needs
passes through this exact sequence:

   1.  INDENT PREPARATION
       Operations/Inventory raises a PR (Purchase Requisition) or SPR
       (Service Purchase Requisition) with line-item detail: part name,
       spec, UOM, qty required, stock on hand, lead time, required-by date.

   2.  3-LEVEL APPROVAL
       The indent is signed up the chain (Indenter → Forwarder → Director)
       before it can proceed to sourcing.

   3.  VENDOR ONBOARDING
       If no suitable vendor exists in the Vendor Directory, a new vendor
       is registered (name, GST, contact, address, category).

   4.  COMPARATIVE QUOTATIONS
       Multiple vendor quotes are collected and laid side by side in the
       Quotation Comparative screen (separate PR vs SPR column sets) to
       identify the best rate, lead time, and terms.

   5.  TC TO THE HO (Technical Comparison to Head Office)
       The comparative analysis is forwarded to Head Office for technical
       review of the proposed vendor/spec choice.

   6.  NFA TO THE DIRECTOR (Note For Approval)
       A formal note is raised to the Director seeking final sanction to
       proceed with the selected vendor and price.

   7.  PO / WO PREPARATION & UPLOAD
       Once sanctioned, a Purchase Order (goods) or Work Order (services)
       is drafted and uploaded into the system.

   8.  PO ISSUED & FORWARDED
       The PO/WO is formally issued to the vendor and forwarded internally
       for tracking.

   9.  PO ACCEPTANCE & TIMELINE PROVISION FROM VENDOR
       Vendor acknowledges the PO and commits a delivery/completion
       timeline.

  10.  PROFORMA (IF NECESSARY)
       For advance-payment vendors, a proforma invoice is collected before
       dispatch.

  11.  INVOICE REGISTRY
       On delivery/completion, the vendor's tax invoice is logged into the
       Invoice Register.

  12.  PAYMENT REQUEST GENERATED
       Accounts (or the requesting department) raises a payment request
       against the registered invoice.

  13.  2-LEVEL APPROVAL ON PAYMENT REQUEST
       Admin Ops approval, then Director approval. THIS is the step where
       the correct BUDGET LINE ITEM (from Stage 4) is selected for the
       payment.

       ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
       │  THE CRITICAL LINK:                                         │
       │  The moment a line item is selected and the payment request │
       │  is forwarded, the amount moves into "Amount in Pipeline"   │
       │  against that budget line. Once payment is finally settled, │
       │  it either:                                                 │
       │    (a) shows as SAVINGS — if the actual rate/amount paid    │
       │        is lower than what was budgeted, or                 │
       │    (b) is recorded as straight UTILIZATION — if it matches  │
       │        or exceeds the budgeted figure.                      │
       │  This single action is what keeps Stage 4's budget triplet  │
       │  (Savings / Pipeline / Utilized) live and accurate.         │
       └─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

  14.  RAISING PRR (Payment Receipt Request / Release Request)
       The formal request to release funds is raised post-approval.

  15.  PAYMENT CONFIRMATION
       Accounts confirms the payment has been executed.

  16.  UTR UPLOADING
       The bank's Unique Transaction Reference (UTR) number is uploaded
       as proof of payment — closing the audit loop for that transaction.

  17.  (Sequence continues to GRN)

  18.  GRN ISSUING (Goods Receipt Note)
       The field/store team confirms physical receipt and counts the
       delivered goods, formally closing the goods-movement side of the
       transaction.

  19.  PO COMPLETION OR AMENDMENT (IF NECESSARY)
       The PO is marked complete, or — if quantities/specs/timelines
       changed mid-cycle — formally amended and re-confirmed.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

WHY THIS MATTERS
  19 discrete, individually-approved steps sound heavy — but at the scale of
  12,000 acres, this is precisely the friction needed to prevent the two
  biggest procurement risks: (1) money committed against a budget line that
  has no headroom left, and (2) goods/services paid for but never verified
  as received. Step 13 is the linchpin — it is the single moment where
  operational spend reconciles against financial planning in real time.



════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
STAGE 8 — VEHICLE ONBOARDING
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Once core equipment and consumables are flowing through the procurement
cycle above, vehicles are onboarded the same way — through Vehicle
Management / Fleet Chart — capturing registration, type, assigned
driver, fuel card linkage, and odometer baseline. Vehicles then become
allocatable resources for both the Cultivation Calendar (Stage 9) and
On-Demand Tasks.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

WHY THIS MATTERS
  Vehicles are the highest-leakage asset class in field agriculture (fuel
  misuse, unauthorized trips, under-utilization). Onboarding them as tracked,
  allocatable resources — rather than informally — is what makes the later
  Fuel Request approval chain and Fleet Chart utilization tracking possible.



════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
STAGE 9 — SCOPE OF WORK, LABOUR, CULTIVATION MASTER → PLAN → CALENDAR
            → ON-DEMAND TASKS
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

With money, land, people, equipment, and vehicles all onboarded, the final
layer is allocation — turning static resources into an actual working
season.

  STEP A — SCOPE OF WORK PER LAND PARCEL
    The last manual allocation: deciding which vendor/activity scope
    applies to which specific land parcel, and distributing available
    labour across parcels.

  STEP B — CULTIVATION MASTER
    A standard, crop-specific, week-wise activity template is defined once
    (e.g. "Napier: Week 1 land prep, Week 3 planting, Week 8 first
    weeding..."). This is the reusable blueprint, not yet tied to any
    specific farm.

  STEP C — CULTIVATION PLAN
    The master template is mapped onto an actual farm/crop combination,
    converting "Week 3" into a real calendar date for that specific land.

  STEP D — CULTIVATION CALENDAR  ★ The signature capability of the system
    This is where the Cultivation Plan becomes an executable, resource-
    allocated schedule across the entire land bank.

    THE CORE CLAIM: building this schedule manually — assigning every
    activity, on every plot, across thousands of acres, to the right dates,
    with the right labour and equipment — is normally a STRATEGY exercise
    that takes a planner roughly 30 MINUTES per plan, and even then only
    produces the high-level strategy, not the granular day-by-day schedule.
    The Cultivation Calendar engine compresses this into roughly 30 SECONDS,
    generating the full granular schedule directly — not just the strategy.
    This is a ~60x time compression on the single most labour-intensive
    planning task in the operation.

    Once generated, the Calendar:
      • Allocates tasks to the appropriate VEHICLES and EQUIPMENT
        (sourced from Stage 8) — every such allocation is recorded as a
        LAND INVESTMENT entry against that farm.
      • Distributes tasks across PLOTS, not raw land parcels — using the
        plot boundaries drawn in Farm Directory's plotting tool combined
        with the crop type assigned to each plot. (A 50-acre land parcel
        with 3 different plots growing 3 different crops gets 3 different
        activity schedules, not one blanket schedule.)
      • Provides live task MONITORING per activity:
          - Image/photo evidence of task completion
          - Swap date tracking (when a scheduled date is moved)
          - Overdue flagging (task past its scheduled date, not done)
          - Underdue flagging (task completed ahead of schedule)

  STEP E — ON-DEMAND TASKS
    For work that falls outside the standard cultivation schedule
    (emergency pest control, ad-hoc repairs, one-off services), the
    On-Demand Task module allows allocation directly at the LAND PARCEL
    level (rather than the plot level used by the Calendar). Each
    allocation schema tracks owner_name, quantity, and farm-by-farm
    assignment, with a status of pending / partial / completed — so partial
    fulfilment across multiple parcels is visible, not just a binary
    done/not-done flag.

  STEP F — INVENTORY LEDGER DUALITY
    Supporting all of the above, Inventory maintains two parallel ledgers
    for every transaction:
      • PER-ITEM LEDGER — full movement history of a single SKU across
        every store/location it has touched.
      • PER-STORE LEDGER — full movement history of everything that has
        moved through a single physical store/location.
    This dual view lets the same stock movement be audited either by
    "what happened to this item" or "what happened in this warehouse,"
    without maintaining two separate datasets.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

WHY THIS MATTERS
  This final stage is where every preceding stage of the workflow physically
  manifests on the ground. The Cultivation Calendar's 30-minute-to-30-second
  compression is not a UX nicety — at 12,000 acres, manual scheduling at
  scale is simply not done today by competitors; it is either skipped
  (leading to ad-hoc, reactive farming) or done at a strategic level only,
  never a per-plot level. Plot-level (not parcel-level) allocation means
  mixed-cropping parcels are handled correctly. The Overdue/Underdue
  monitoring closes the loop back to Stage 4's budget tracking — every
  vehicle/equipment allocation here becomes a land investment entry that
  rolls back up into the per-acre investment figure the Director monitors.



════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
THE COMPLETE CHAIN — ONE LINE PER STAGE
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

  Budget (Capex/Opex)
      → Amortization (loan terms on Capex)
          → Cash Flow (monthly inflow/outflow incl. EMI)
              → Finalized Budget (Savings / Pipeline / Utilized per line item)
                  → Land Onboarding (Leads → KYC → Registration, multi-owner/
                    multi-parcel aware)
                  → HR Onboarding (MRF against budget line → 2-level approval
                    → staffing within allocated envelope)
                  → Equipment/Inventory (19-step Indent → Approval → Vendor →
                    Quotation → TC/NFA → PO → Invoice → 2-level Payment
                    Approval [budget line selected here] → PRR → UTR → GRN →
                    PO Closure)
                  → Vehicle Onboarding
                      → Scope of Work + Labour Distribution per parcel
                          → Cultivation Master (template)
                              → Cultivation Plan (template → real farm/dates)
                                  → Cultivation Calendar (full schedule,
                                    plot-level, vehicle/equipment-allocated,
                                    monitored — generated in ~30 seconds)
                                  → On-Demand Tasks (ad-hoc, parcel-level
                                    allocation, partial-fulfilment aware)

Every box in this chain writes back into the budget's Savings / Pipeline /
Utilized figures and ultimately into the per-farm investment ledger — closing
the loop from financial plan to physical execution to financial actual.
